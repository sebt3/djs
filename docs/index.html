Howto

The following slides show you the html code to use this

Base template

<html>
  <head>
    <title>title</title>
    <link rel="stylesheet" href="https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/sebt3/djs@0.3.0/dist/djs.min.css">
  </head>
  <body>
    <div class="jslides">
      <div class="slides">
        <section>
        First slide content here
        </section>
      </div>
    </div>
    <script src="https://d3js.org/d3.v4.min.js"></script>
    <script src="https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/sebt3/djs@0.3.0/dist/djs.min.js"></script>
  </body>
</html>

A title slide

        <section class="first">
          <div class="content">
            <h1>djs</h1>
            <h2>Documentation</h2>
            <small>A very light javascript framework for presentation</small>
	  </div>
        </section>

A slide with title

        <section>
          <div class="title"><div>
            <h1>A title</h1>
            <h2>A sub title</h2>
          </div></div>
          Slide content
        </section>

TOC

  • A table of content slide is just a section classed "index".
  • You're free to add your title if you need.
        <section class="index">
          <div class="title"><div>
            <h1>your TOC title</h1>
          </div></div>
        </section>

split the slide in parts

        <section>
          <div class='full'>
           <div class='row'>Content on top</div>
           <div class='row'>
            <div class='panel'>Content on the left</div>
            <div class='panel'>Content on the right</div>
           </div>
          </div>
        </section>
Content on the left

speaker notes

  • Speaker notes are only available in the speaker view
  • It's just a matter of adding a "notes" classed div in the slide's section.
        <section>
          Slide content
          <div class="notes">
           <p>Your notes here</p>
          </div>
        </section>
